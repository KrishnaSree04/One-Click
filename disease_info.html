Headaches are a very common condition that most people will experience many times during their lives. A headache is a pain in your head or face that’s often described as a pressure that’s throbbing, constant, sharp or dull. While most headaches aren’t dangerous, certain types can be a sign of a more serious condition.

Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, often due to an underlying illness or infection. It is a natural response by the body's immune system to help fight off infections. Most fevers are not dangerous and can be managed at home. Getting plenty of rest, staying hydrated, and taking over-the-counter fever-reducing medications can help.

Symptoms: High Temperataure ,body pains ,headache ,Dehydration ,Irritability and Loss of appetite.
Prevention: Be Clean and Hygiene ,Avoid Close Contact ,Stay Hydrated and maintain healthy body and food.
Vitamin Deficiencies: Vitamin D , C , A

The common cold, often referred to simply as a cold, is a viral respiratory infection that affects the nose and throat. It is one of the most prevalent illnesses in the world and can occur throughout the year, although it is more common in the fall and winter months.

Symptoms: Sore throat ,Watery Eyes ,Sneezing and coughing ,mild aches.
Prevention: Be Clean and Hygiene ,Avoid Close Contact ,Stay Hydrated and maintain healthy body and food ,good Immune system.
Vitamin Deficiencies: Vitamin D , C , A and zinc.

Type 1 diabetes is a chronic autoimmune disease in which the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. Type 1 diabetes often develops in childhood or adolescence, but it can occur at any age.

Symptoms: Excessive thirst ,Frequent urination ,Unexplained weight loss ,Blurred vision ,Increased appetite
Prevention: Early Detection , Monitoring the disease ,Good care towards health and haviing a well balanced diet.
Vitamin Deficiencies: Vitamin D , B12 , C and B6.

Type 2 diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high levels of blood sugar (glucose) in the body. Unlike Type 1 diabetes, where the immune system attacks the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas, Type 2 diabetes typically involves a combination of insulin resistance and impaired insulin production.

Symptoms: Excessive thirst ,Frequent urination ,Unexplained weight loss or weight gain ,Blurred vision ,Slow wound healing ,recurrent infections.
Prevention: Maintain a Healthy Weight ,Adopt a Balanced Diet ,Get Quality Sleep ,Regular Check-ups and Stress Management.
Vitamin Deficiencies: Vitamin D , B12 , C and Magnesium.

Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can also target other parts of the body. It is caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. TB is a significant global health concern due to its high prevalence and the potential for severe illness and death if left untreated.

Symptoms: Persistent Cough ,Chest Pain ,Weight Loss ,Coughing Up Blood ,Night Sweats and Light Fever.
Prevention: Vaccination ,Screening and Testing ,HIV Prevention and Care ,Good health care and being Hygienic.
Vitamin Deficiencies: Vitamin D , B Complex , A and E.
